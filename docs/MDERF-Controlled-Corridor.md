## Overview

The MDERF Controlled Corridor model defines a constrained deployment strategy for mobile energy reception systems under real-world safety, efficiency, and governance limitations.

Rather than targeting open public environments, this model prioritizes **controlled and semi-enclosed infrastructure zones**.

---

## Rationale

Initial exploration of open highway-based directed energy transmission revealed critical limitations:

- Safety risks in uncontrolled environments
- Inefficiencies in energy transmission under mobility and exposure constraints
- High infrastructure complexity and regulatory barriers

This module refines the deployment pathway toward **viable operational domains**.

---

## Controlled Corridor Definition

A Controlled Corridor is defined as:

> A physically and operationally constrained pathway where environmental variables, access, and safety conditions can be actively managed.

---

## Key Characteristics

- Restricted or monitored access
- Limited human/animal exposure
- Predictable vehicle flow
- Controlled structural environment

---

## Candidate Deployment Environments

### 1. Industrial Logistics Zones
- Ports
- Container yards
- Warehousing corridors

---

### 2. Military and Secured Facilities
- Base transportation routes
- Autonomous logistics corridors

---

### 3. Disaster Response Infrastructure
- Temporary energy corridors
- Emergency vehicle support routes

---

### 4. Airport and Ground Operations
- Service vehicle pathways
- Controlled airside environments

---

## System Role

Within these environments, MDERF operates as:

- A **supplemental energy system**
- Not a primary propulsion replacement
- A controlled augmentation layer to existing energy systems

---

## Safety Principle

The Controlled Corridor model prioritizes:

- Environmental containment
- Conditional activation
- Exposure minimization

---

## Strategic Positioning

This module establishes:

> WHERE the system should be deployed  
not HOW it is implemented

---

## Non-Claim Statement

This module does not introduce new transmission physics or hardware systems.

It defines:

> A deployment constraint model for safe and viable system integration
